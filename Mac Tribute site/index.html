Mac Miller - American Rapper

(Also known as Malcolm James McCormick)

Malcolm James McCormick (January 19, 1992 â€“ September 7, 2018), known professionally as Mac Miller, was an American rapper and record producer. Miller began his career in Pittsburgh's hip hop scene in 2007, at the age of fifteen. Miller struggled with addiction and substance abuse, which was often referenced in his lyrics. On September 7, 2018, Miller died from an accidental drug overdose of cocaine, fentanyl, and alcohol at his home, aged 26.

Albums

Blue Slide Park

Blue Slide Park was the debut studio album that was released on November 8, 2011 by Rostrum Records. It debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200.

Watching Movies with Sound Off

Watching Movies with Sound Off was released on June 18, 2013 by Rostrum Records. This album featured Earl Sweatshirt, Ab-Soul, Action Bronson, Schoolboy Q, and Tyler, the Creator and others.

GO:OD AM

GO:OD AM was released on September 18, 2015 by REMember Music and Warner Bros. Records. It featured Ab-Soul, Chief Keef, Lil B, Miguel, and Little Dragon.

The Divine Feminine

The Divine Feminine was Mac's 4th album, released on September 16, 2016. It featured Kendrick Lamar, Anderson .Paak, Ty Dolla Sign, Ariana Grande, and others.

Swimming

Swimming was relased on August 3, 2018. The album had no features, but contained vocal contributions by Snoop Dogg, Thundercat, and others. It is the final album released during his lifetime. Mac Miller died on September 7, 2018.

Circles

Circles was posthumously released on January 17, 2020. It was the final album that Mac Miller was working on before his death. Production was completed by Jon Brion.
